The Second Era 1983-1992

Honda returned to Formula 1 in 1983 as an engine supplier for Spirit. It wasn't until the 1984 Dallas Grand Prix, where Keke Rosberg, driving for William-Honda, won the race, and put the paddock on notice with regard to the Honda engines. In 1985, Nigel Mansell, also driving for Williams-Honda, won the last 3 races of the season, and the following year, in 1986, won the constructors title, which began a 6 year streak of Honda powered teams winning the drivers and constructors titles. The most dominant season was 1988, where Alain Prost and Ayrton Senna, racing for McLaren-Honda, won 15 of 16 races, and 15 of 16 pole posisions, the constructors championship for McLaren, and Senna's first driver's championship.
