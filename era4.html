The Fourth Era 2015-Present

Honda returned for the fourth, and currently final time in 2015 with McLaren. The second partnership was nowhere near as fruitful as the first one. The Honda engine was constrained by the McLaren size zero concept, and suffered from the witch's brew of being woefully underpowered, as well as woefully unreliable. The pair split in 2018, with McLaren going to Renault engines, and Honda went to Toro Rosso. After Toro Rosso's showing in the 2018 season, the main Red Bull team also took on Honda engines, replacing their Tag Heur branded Renault engines. In 2021, Honda received its first championship since returning, with the Red Bull RB16B, and Max Verstappen. In 2022, after officially withdrawing from F1 in 2021, Honda once again won the driver's championship with Max Verstappen, but this time, Red Bull racing also won the constructor's championship.
