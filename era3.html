The Third Era 2000-2008

Honda returned once more in 2000 as the engine supplier for British American Racing (BAR). BAR-Honda would be a works factory deal, with Honda providing the engines to BAR for free, with full factory support. Honda would also provide free engines to Jordan Grand Prix in 2001 and 2002. Despite outperforming BAR, in 2003, Jordan would be dropped in favour of BAR, and Honda would purchase British American Tobacco(BAT)'s remaining 55% in shares to become the full owner of the team. BAT would continue to sponsor with the Lucky Strike brand, and as such Lucky Strike BAR-Honda became Lucky Strike Honda Racing F1 Team. Honda would continue on until 2008, where the global financial crisis once again prompted them to withdraw from the sport, unwilling to continue with the team's 300 million US dollar budget. The team would be bought out by Ross Brawn for 2009, and compete as Brawn GP, with Mercedes engines. Brawn GP would later be bought out by Mercedes, and become the team we now know as Mercedes-AMG Petronas F1 Team.
