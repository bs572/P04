The First Era 1964-1968

Honda joined Formula 1 for the first time in 1964, 4 years after producing their first road car. During this time, Honda's all Japanese garage, with the exception of their drivers was a rarity in the European Dominated sport. They achieved their first victory in 1965, during their second year of competition. After a winless 1966 season, the Honda RA300 won its first race, the 1966 Italian Grand Prix. The 1968 RA301 suffered from reliability issues, and its sucessor, the RA302 only appeared in a single race, before a fiery crash killed driver Jo Schlesser, prompting Honda to withdraw from Formula 1.
